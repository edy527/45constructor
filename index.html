Drywall

Any of various large rigid sheets of finishing material used in drywall construction to face the interior walls of dwellings and other buildings.
Drywall construction is the application of walls without the use of mortar or plaster

Skim Coat

Is a texturing technique used to make a wall smooth. Drywallers use this technique to hide an imperfect taping job, to give the wall a plaster-like appearance in situations when only the smoothest surface will do. Skim coated is the only way to archieve a level 5 drywall finish, which a number of trade associations, including the Painting and Decorating Constractors of America recommend for areas of bright or critical lighting.

Cover Coat

Used for filling and smoothing above-grade, monolithic interior concrete ceilings and columns, embedding paper drywall tape or acting as the first fill coat on bead, trim and fasteners. The compound dries white for a quick, easy finish.
